A Lightweight Web3D Digital Twin Framework for Real-Time ESG Monitoring Using IoT Sensors

Thepparit Sinthamrongruk Keshav Dahal Napat Harnpornchai
Electronics, Vol. 15, No. 8, pp. 1736 — MDPI

This study proposes a lightweight Web3D-based digital twin framework for real-time ESG monitoring in smart buildings. The system integrates an independently developed IoT sensor network with a browser-native 3D visualization platform, enabling real-time monitoring of ESG indicators—including electricity consumption—without requiring proprietary software or dedicated rendering hardware. Validated through a 12-month real-world deployment with 60 IoT sensors, achieving 66 FPS rendering, 78 ms latency, and 98% sensor data consistency.

Effects of Web-Based Physical–Cognitive Telerehabilitation Exergaming on Physical and Cognitive Performance in Chronic Stroke Survivors: Protocol for a Randomized Controlled Trial

Puntarik Keawtep Sirinun Boripuntakul Piangkwan Sa-Nguanmoo Pranglada Jearjaroen Cattaleeya Sittichoke Teerawat Kamnardsiri Thepparit Sinthamrongruk Somporn Sungkarat
Journal of Clinical Medicine, Vol. 15, No. 8, pp. 2945 — MDPI

This paper presents a protocol for a two-arm randomized controlled trial examining the effectiveness of a web-based physical–cognitive telerehabilitation exergaming program on motor function, balance, gait speed, fall risk, cognition, and enjoyment in chronic stroke survivors. Fifty-two participants will be randomly assigned to a 12-week intervention or a usual-care control group, with blinded assessors evaluating both physical and cognitive domains.

Exploring Tourist Behavior and Usability Improvement for Smart Tourism in Secondary Cities: A Case Study of Lamphun Province

Pimsiri Suangswang Thepparit Sinthamrongruk Chalermpon Kongjit Korawan Sangkakorn
2026 ECTI DAMT & NCON, pp. 597–602 — IEEE

This study explores tourist behavior and usability improvement for tourism applications in Thailand's secondary cities, focusing on Lamphun Province as a pilot case. Data from 50 tourists who used or expressed interest in tram-based sightseeing services were analyzed using the 5A's of Tourism framework (Attraction, Accessibility, Accommodation, Amenities, and Activities) and ISO 9241-11 usability standards. Results show that most visitors travel for leisure and cultural purposes. Attraction and Accessibility performed strongly, while Activities and Amenities emerged as key areas needing enhancement. Usability analysis showed that Satisfaction scored slightly lower than Effectiveness and Efficiency, highlighting the need to strengthen engagement and emotional appeal. Correlation analysis identified exploratory associations between Accessibility and Satisfaction, and between Activities and both Satisfaction and Effectiveness. Based on these findings, four priority areas—Accessibility, Activities, Accommodation, and Amenities— were identified for design and functional improvement. Integrating the 5A's model with Design Thinking and usability frameworks offers a practical approach for developing user-centered smart tourism applications in secondary cities.

A Discrete-Event Simulation Approach to Evaluate Smart Parking Policies for Carbon Reduction: A Case Study of a Thai University

Peeradech Wongkum Chalermpon Kongjit Thepparit Sinthamrongruk
2026 ECTI DAMT & NCON, pp. 418–423 — IEEE

This paper evaluates smart parking policies using discrete-event simulation of a Chiang Mai University campus parking lot. Four scenarios were modeled: traditional parking (S0), capacity expansion (S1), LED status signs (S2), and a reservation system (S3). Results show that S2 (LED signs) reduced emissions by 13.1% compared to baseline, while S1 increased emissions by 8.5% despite achieving the highest success rate (90%). Weighted evaluation ranked S2 highest for sustainability trade-offs.

Virtual Reality-Based Simulation for Earthquake Survival Training in Buildings

Thepparit Sinthamrongruk Khongthat Thongphun Chiawat Palee Tachanat Akarajaka
2025 9th International Conference on Information Technology (InCIT), pp. 302–306

This study develops an earthquake simulation program using Virtual Reality (VR) technology to enhance users' knowledge and survival skills. A pilot study with 20 participants showed that the VR group achieved a 90% survival rate compared to 60% in the manual-learning group, with over 30% less head damage. These findings demonstrate that VR training enhances practical learning, reduces anxiety, builds confidence, and supports effective decision-making in emergencies.

Development of an Automated Pill Dispenser Utilizing Image Processing

Thepparit Sinthamrongruk Wasin Wongkum Thishnapha Vudhironarit
2025 SKIMA, Paisley, United Kingdom, pp. 1–6

This paper presents an automated pill dispensing system leveraging image processing and AI for accurate pill detection and classification. A custom dataset — the Thailand Medicine Packaging Dataset — was developed, comprising 1,020 annotated images representing blister packs, strip packages, and alu-alu packaging. The dataset was utilized to train a YOLOv11-based deep convolutional neural network for pill localization within the packaging, classifying each cell as Full or Empty.
